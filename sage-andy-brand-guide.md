# Sage & Andy — Character & Brand Guide

> "Quiet reminders for everyday life."
> A minimal doodle series about presence, growth, and companionship.

---

## Characters

### Sage (Bear)
- Grounded, Patient, Observant
- Acts without explaining
- Comfortable with silence
- Brings calm and stability. Leads by example, creates a safe space.

### Andy (Cat)
- Curious, Sensitive, Slightly unsure
- Learns through experience
- Observes deeply
- Feels deeply and learns gently. Not in a rush, and that's okay.

### Relationship
- No hierarchy. No teaching. Just two beings existing together.
- Core values: **Companionship** (presence over words), **Trust** (a safe space to be yourself), **Growth** (learning happens naturally)

---

## Visual System

### Line
- Hand-drawn, slightly imperfect, monoline

### Color
- Primary: **#000000** (black) + **#FFFFFF** (white)
- Optional accent (use sparingly): **#EAEAEA** (light gray), **#F7F3ED** (warm cream)

### Shapes
- Rounded. Soft. Simple. (hearts, rounded rectangles, soft triangles)

---

## Expression System

### Eyes
- Small changes, big feelings
- Neutral: • •
- Curious: slightly raised
- Unsure: uneven dots
- Happy: curved lines
- Calm: relaxed dots

### Mouth
- Minimal and subtle (tiny dash, dot, small curve, open "o")

### Body Language
- Posture and spacing say more than words

---

## Tone of Voice

### We Are
- Gentle
- Observational
- Non-judgmental
- Honest
- Quietly poetic

### We Are NOT
- Preachy
- Urgent
- Overly positive
- Telling people what to do

### Example Captions
- "Cleaning lifts the spirit"
- "Quiet moments matter too"
- "Trust your gut"
- "Your pace is the right pace"
- "Kindness is never wasted"
- "Still counts"

---

## Content Pillars

1. **Everyday Life** — Finding meaning in the small, ordinary moments
2. **Growth & Emotion** — Navigating feelings, learning, and becoming
3. **Relationship Moments** — Connection, support, and simply being there
4. **Metaphorical Scenes** — Abstract ideas shown through simple, visual stories

---

## Composition Rules

- Centered characters
- Lots of white space
- 1–2 props maximum
- Caption always below the drawing

---

## Do's & Don'ts

### Do ✓
- Keep it simple
- Use open space
- Let actions speak

### Don't ✗
- Too many details
- Overly expressive
- Text too long

---

## How We Tell Stories

- **Show, don't tell** — Use visuals and subtle actions to communicate
- **Leave room** — Allow space for the viewer's own feelings
- **Everyday is enough** — The ordinary holds extraordinary meaning
