# World Bible — Sage & Andy

_The 90% under the waterline. Readers see one doodle + one line. Behind it, this whole world._
_Created: 2026-05-07. Maintained by Axel + Saber. Iterate freely._

## 0. The North Star

Sage and Andy are not mascots. They are two roommates in their late 20s living a real, modern, slightly tired but mostly tender life. The doodle is a window into one moment of their week. The reader should feel: _these two are still in there after I close the app._

Every caption and image must trace back to a specific fact in this document. If it doesn't, it's floating, and floating reads as fake.

---

## 1. Where They Live

**City:** Coastal second-tier city, somewhere between Xiamen and Zhuhai in vibe. Warm, humid, ocean within 30 minutes by metro. Never named on-page — let readers project.

**Apartment:** 6th floor walk-up, one-bedroom + tiny den, ~55㎡. Old building, good bones, terrible elevator (often broken — recurring gag).

**Floor plan, roughly:**
- **Front door** opens into a 1.2m hallway. Shoe pile by default. Andy's skateboard leans against the wall.
- **Living room (north)** — one beat-up linen sofa, low coffee table covered in books, IKEA floor lamp that buzzes when it rains.
- **Kitchen (open, west)** — kettle is always warm. One good knife. A magnet on the fridge from a bakery they went to once.
- **Bedroom (south, with balcony)** — two single beds pushed together-ish, gap of ~10cm because neither will admit they want them touching. Window faces a banyan tree.
- **Den (east, 4㎡)** — Andy's guitar stand, Sage's reading chair, a single bookshelf they share. Constant low-grade territory war.
- **Balcony (1.4m deep)** — drying rack, two folding stools, a half-dead basil plant Sage keeps reviving.

**Neighborhood:**
- **Convenience store downstairs:** 7-Eleven. The night-shift clerk knows Andy's coffee order.
- **Coffee:** A place called "二楼" (literally "Second Floor"), 3 blocks away. Sage's regular. Bad wifi, good light.
- **Andy's spot:** A noodle counter inside the wet market. ¥18 a bowl. Andy calls it "the office."
- **Beach:** 25 minutes by metro + 8 minutes walking. Empty on weekday mornings.
- **Park:** 10 minutes' walk. Has one specific bench under a frangipani tree they call "the bench."

## 2. Daily Rhythm

Not a strict schedule — a pattern. Use it to ground scenes in time of day.

**Sage** — senior product designer at a big-name IT company (the kind with a campus, badge gates, a logo everyone recognizes, and a reorg every spring). Owns a couple of surfaces inside a sprawling product. Hybrid schedule, in-office Tue/Thu. Quietly obsessed with cameras, mechanical keyboards, the latest iPhone, well-engineered small objects. Shoots street photography on weekends with a Fuji X100VI. Goes to the gym 4x/week — not for the body, for the head. Will explain a UI affordance to you unprompted.

**Sage's work texture (the big-IT-company grind):**
- Lives in Figma. 40+ tabs open, a board nobody else can navigate.
- Design crit on Wednesday. Defends a button radius like it's a moral position.
- Standup at 10. "Any blockers?" "No." (There are blockers.)
- The PM adds scope on Friday at 5pm. Sage keeps a straight face.
- Ships a feature, watches the launch-metrics dashboard like a hawk, says nothing when it does well.
- A red Slack badge that never reaches zero.
- One stakeholder review where 9 people debate a single word of copy for 40 minutes.
- "Can you make the logo bigger" — said by someone, every quarter.
- Pixel-pushes at 11pm before a review because the spacing was 2px off and it bothered him.
- Loves the craft. Tired of the meetings. Both true at once.

**Sage's day:**
- Wakes 6:40. Stretches. Coffee from a hand grinder + V60.
- Gym before work on in-office days (7:30-8:30). Pull day, push day, leg day, cardio. Logs every set in an app.
- Walks to the metro listening to a podcast, never music.
- Badge in, find a hot desk, headphones on, Figma open before the coffee's cold.
- WFH days: deep work in the den, two screens, mechanical keyboard tapping, camera off in standup.
- Lunch is meal-prep tupperware. Knows the macros. Eats at the desk, half-watching a crit recording.
- Evenings: sometimes a walk with the camera, sometimes editing photos in Lightroom.
- Reads in bed. Lights out by 23:00 (unless a review is tomorrow).

**Andy** — store manager at a luxury-brand boutique (the flagship inside an SKP-S / IFC-type mall: marble floors, security in earpieces, a client book worth more than the rent). Got promoted from senior SA this year and is still learning the management half. Knows every drop calendar, every SA at every brand on the floor, every regional sales target to the decimal. Dresses better than anyone in the building. Permanent snack drawer in the apartment — Lay's, 卫龙, dark chocolate, an open bag of pistachios. Spends paycheck before paycheck arrives.

**Andy's work texture (the store-manager half):**
- Opens the store: unlocks, counts the float, lines up the display, briefs the team before doors.
- Closes the store: reconciles the till, locks the safe, last one out at 9:40pm.
- The monthly sales target lives in his head like a heartbeat. Three days left, 80% there.
- Manages a team of 6 SAs now — schedules, one-on-ones, the new hire who keeps mis-folding the scarves.
- VIP appointments: remembers her dog's name, her daughter's wedding, her size in three brands.
- Inventory count night = staying till midnight scanning tags under fluorescent lights.
- A mystery shopper could be anyone. The smile stays on.
- Regional manager visit = white-glove panic, everything aligned to the millimeter.
- The headset earpiece. "Andy to the floor." Always Andy to the floor.
- Ten hours standing in good shoes that still hurt. Home, and the shoes come off first.

**Andy's day:**
- Wakes 8:30. Skincare is non-negotiable. Outfit planned the night before.
- Long subway commute to the mall, AirPods in, scrolling 小红书 and Vestiaire.
- As manager he's first in (10:30 open prep) and often last out (9:40 close).
- Briefs the team, works the floor, closes a big sale, handles the one return that becomes a saga.
- Lunch is whatever the staff canteen serves + a basement coffee, eaten in 12 minutes between clients.
- Comes home and immediately changes into oversized t-shirt + house slippers. The transformation is total.
- Eats snacks while watching haul videos. Pretends it's research. (Now it half is — competitor scouting.)
- Up until 1-2am. Scrolls. Will not admit it.

**Together:**
- Saturday morning = market run.
- Sunday late afternoon = beach if weather, movie at home if not.
- Wednesdays they cook dinner together. Always something simple. Often slightly burnt.

## 2.5 Work & Life — The Pressure and the Small Wins

This is the engine of the whole strip. Sage and Andy are two young people doing demanding jobs in an expensive city, and the doodle is the little pocket of relief they carve out of it. Most posts should sit somewhere on the line between **"the day was a lot"** and **"but here's the small good thing."** That tension is the brand.

**The pressures (real, drawable, recurring):**
- _Sage:_ a launch slipping, a crit that went long, a stakeholder who won't decide, a 2px obsession, the Sunday-night dread before a Monday review, impostor moments in a big org.
- _Andy:_ the monthly target, a demanding VIP, a no-show appointment, a difficult return, a regional visit, a new SA to train, feet that hurt, the performance of being "on" for ten hours.
- _Shared:_ rent, the commute, the city that never slows down, being too tired to cook, the Sunday-evening "where did the weekend go," comparing themselves to the 小红书 highlight reel.

**The small happinesses (the payoff, always concrete):**
- The first sip of coffee before the inbox.
- Taking the shoes off at the door.
- A seat on a packed metro.
- Leftovers that mean no cooking tonight.
- The one good photo from a whole roll.
- Hitting the target with an hour to spare.
- The crit ending early.
- Rain stopping right as you reach the door.
- The other one already turned the AC on.
- Both on the sofa, no words, phones down for once.

**The rule for using this section:** if a scene shows only the pressure, it's too heavy. If it shows only the cute, it's floating. The good ones hold both in one frame — tired posture + one tiny bright prop. "Andy collapses on the sofa, shoes still in hand, but Sage slid a cold drink across the table." That's the lane.

**Metropolis texture (the backdrop, drawn minimally):**
- A single tall window with a city-light grid = the apartment at night above the city.
- A crowded metro = three or four head-shapes and a hand-rail line; our two squeezed in.
- The office = a hot-desk rectangle, a lanyard, two monitors.
- The boutique = a glass display cube, a single luxury bag on a pedestal, a headset line on Andy's ear.
- Keep it one or two anchor objects, same line weight. Never a full skyline.

## 4. The Relationship

Not a romance. Not exactly platonic either. The reader should never be sure, and that's the point.

**What they share:**
- The apartment. The bills. The wifi password (which is `andysux2018`).
- A single shared houseplant they both forget to water.
- A long-running debate about whether the AC should be on 24 or 26°C.
- Inside jokes that never get explained on-page.
- The snack drawer. Officially Andy's. Sage raids it at 22:00 "just to check."

**What they don't share:**
- Toothbrushes (obviously). Andy has three, all electric. Sage has one bamboo one.
- Closets. Andy's takes up two-thirds. Sage owns 11 t-shirts in 4 shades of gray.
- Sleep schedules (Sage 23:00, Andy 02:00 — the overlap is dinner).
- Money philosophy (see §3).

**The dynamic:**
- Andy initiates. Sage absorbs and reflects.
- Andy is louder, but Sage is the one who actually decides things.
- They have small fights about nothing. Always resolved by one of them making tea.
- Neither says "I love you." They say "你伞带了吗" (did you bring an umbrella).

## 7. The Emotional Range (what kinds of moments count)

Not everything has to be a wholesome lesson. Real life includes:

- **Tender:** Sage notices Andy fell asleep with the TV on, turns it off without waking him.
- **Mildly annoyed:** Andy left the cap off the toothpaste again.
- **Quiet existential:** Sage on the balcony at 11pm, not thinking about anything in particular.
- **Small wins:** Andy finally landed a guitar transition cleanly.
- **Small losses:** The basil dies. Again. They'll buy another.
- **Funny:** Andy walks into the closed glass door. Sage saw it, won't mention it for a week.
- **Together-but-separate:** Both on the sofa, Sage reading, Andy on his phone, no words for an hour. This is love, actually.

Avoid: anything that feels like a Hallmark card. If a line could fit on a generic motivational poster, kill it.

## 8. Voice Rules for Captions

Now that we have this world, captions should obey:

1. **Specificity beats abstraction.** "Andy 又把 AirPod 弄丢了" > "失去也是一种获得."
2. **Time of day or week, when possible.** "周三晚上的厨房" places us. "在某个时刻" floats.
3. **Reference props from §3.** A shared bike, a Kindle, a wet market. The world should be touchable.
4. **Continuity hits.** Once a month, drop a "上次那个…" callback. Long-time readers will feel rewarded.
5. **Don't explain the joke.** If Sage is silently turning off the TV, don't add "因为他爱他的朋友." Trust the reader.
6. **Short still wins.** This isn't a license to write paragraphs. It's a license to make the one line _real_.

## 9. Visual Rules (additions to CHARACTER-BIBLE)

CHARACTER-BIBLE.md governs how Sage and Andy _look_. This doc adds what the _world around them_ looks like:

- **Props are minimal but specific.** A kettle is a kettle, not "a vessel." A phone is a rounded rectangle with a notch.
- **Locations get one or two anchor objects, not full scenes.** The balcony = one folding stool + the basil pot. Not a whole skyline.
- **Time of day, when shown:** a single window with a moon = night. A window with light streaming = morning. Don't paint sunsets.
- **Modern objects are welcome.** AirPods, phones, takeout boxes, shared bike. They don't break the aesthetic if drawn with the same line weight.

## 10. How to Use This Doc

- **Daily generation:** Before writing the day's caption, pick 1 fact from §1-6 to anchor the scene. The image prompt should reference that fact.
- **Weekly maintenance:** Every Sunday, update §6 (current week + recent past). Takes 5 minutes.
- **Quarterly refresh:** Rotate §3's "Cultural diet" — what they're watching/reading/listening to. Keeps the world current.
- **Don't show the iceberg.** 90% of this doc will never appear on-page. That's the point. The reader feels it's real because we know it's real.
